# Cost Improvement Challenge

## Objective

Analyze the `sample-bill-data.csv` and identify **3 concrete cost improvements** that you would recommend to the engineering team. Document each improvement using the template below.

This exercise simulates a real cost optimization review. You are the architect presenting findings to leadership — be specific, quantify savings, and acknowledge tradeoffs.

---

## Instructions

1. Review the full bill CSV and the analysis questions (use them as a warm-up if needed).
2. Select 3 improvements that collectively deliver meaningful savings.
3. Prioritize improvements that are actionable within 1-2 weeks, not multi-quarter migrations.
4. Fill in the template below for each improvement.
5. Write a brief executive summary (3-4 sentences) at the end.

---

## Improvement Template

Copy this template three times and fill it in:

### Improvement [1/2/3]: [Short Title]

**Current State:**
Describe what exists today — the resource, its configuration, and its current monthly cost.

**Proposed Change:**
Describe exactly what you would change. Be specific about instance types, storage classes, configuration changes, or architectural modifications.

**Estimated Monthly Savings:**
Provide a dollar amount and percentage reduction for this line item. Show your math.

**Risk Assessment:**
What could go wrong? What monitoring would you put in place before or after the change? Rate: Low / Medium / High.

**Implementation Effort:**
How long would this take? What teams need to be involved? Rate: Hours / Days / Weeks.

**Frugal Architect Principle:**
Which of Werner Vogels' Frugal Architect laws does this improvement align with?

---

## Executive Summary

_Write 3-4 sentences summarizing your total projected savings, the risk profile of your recommendations, and the implementation timeline._

---

## Scoring Rubric

Your submission will be evaluated on:

| Criteria | Excellent (5) | Good (3) | Needs Work (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------|----------------|
| **Accuracy** | Savings calculations are correct with realistic assumptions | Minor errors in math or pricing | Significantly wrong estimates or missing calculations |
| **Specificity** | Names exact instance types, storage classes, and configurations | Generally correct direction but vague on specifics | Generic advice like "use smaller instances" |
| **Risk Awareness** | Identifies real risks and proposes monitoring/rollback | Mentions risk but without mitigation plan | Ignores risk entirely |
| **Prioritization** | Targets highest-impact items first; quick wins before big projects | Reasonable prioritization | Random selection without justification |
| **Principle Mapping** | Correctly maps to Frugal Architect laws with explanation of why | Correct law cited but shallow connection | Wrong principle or no mapping |

**Scoring:**
- 20-25 points: Excellent — ready to present to leadership
- 13-19 points: Good — solid analysis with room for depth
- 7-12 points: Developing — revisit the bill data and Frugal Architect principles
- Below 7: Start with the analysis questions first to build familiarity
