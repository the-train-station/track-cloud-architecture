# Lab: The Frugal Architect in Practice

This lab puts Werner Vogels' Frugal Architect principles into practice by analyzing a realistic AWS bill and identifying concrete cost improvements.

## Prerequisites

- Familiarity with common AWS services (EC2, RDS, S3, ElastiCache)
- Understanding of the 7 Laws of The Frugal Architect from the lesson material
- Basic knowledge of AWS pricing models

## Lab Flow

Work through the materials in this order:

### Step 1: Review the Bill (10 min)

Open `sample-bill-data.csv` and familiarize yourself with the account's spending. Note services, instance types, and costs. Get a feel for where money is going.

### Step 2: Answer Analysis Questions (20 min)

Work through `bill-analysis-questions.md`. These questions build progressively from simple cost identification to architectural reasoning. Start with the Easy questions and push into Hard territory.

### Step 3: Complete the Challenge (30 min)

Open `cost-improvement-challenge.md` and identify your top 3 improvements. Use the template provided — specificity matters. Calculate real savings, acknowledge real risks.

### Step 4: Compare with Solution (10 min)

After completing your analysis, review `solution.md` to compare your findings. There is no single correct answer, but the solution covers the highest-impact opportunities and maps each to a Frugal Architect principle.

## What Success Looks Like

You can read an AWS bill critically, distinguish necessary spend from waste, quantify improvement opportunities, and connect cost decisions back to architectural principles.
